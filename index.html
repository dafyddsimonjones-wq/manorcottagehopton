The Cottage

A 300-year-old wing, lovingly restored

Nestled in quiet Hopton hamlet and overlooking Carsington Water, Manor Cottage is a Grade II listed 18th-century wing with original oak beams and a roaring log burner — blending period character with every modern comfort.

A farmhouse kitchen, spacious oak-beamed living and dining areas, four en-suite bedrooms across three floors, and a patio with sweeping rural views make this the perfect base for any group.

Directions & Transport

Getting to Manor Cottage

Hopton village sits at the southern edge of the Peak District, within easy reach of major road and rail connections from across the UK.

Hopton, Wirksworth, Derbyshire · DE4 4DF

By Road

Driving Directions

From the South/M1: Take the A38 north to Ripley, then the B6013 through Ambergate and Whatstandwell, following signs for Wirksworth/Carsington Water. Turn into Hopton village off the B5035.

From the North/Manchester: A6 south through Buxton and Bakewell to Matlock, then the B5023 towards Wirksworth. Hopton is signed from the B5035.

By Rail

Cromford Station

East Midlands Railway (Sheffield–Derby line). Approx. 8 min drive from the cottage. Direct trains from Derby (~35 min) and Nottingham (~50 min).

Matlock Station

East Midlands Railway terminus. Approx. 15 min drive. Direct services from Derby (~45 min). Taxi and bus connections available to Wirksworth.

About the Cottage

Why Libba loves Manor Cottage

“Manor Cottage is a very special place. It’s been in the family for years and I’ve taken great care to restore it sensitively — keeping the original oak beams, the wonderful log-burning fireplace and the authentic period character, while bringing it fully up to modern standards. I genuinely believe there’s nowhere better to experience the southern Peak District. Carsington Water is right on the doorstep, Chatsworth is a short drive, and the walking from the door is exceptional.”

“I welcome families, groups of friends and multi-generational parties. Well-behaved dogs are always welcome too — there’s plenty of outdoor space and wonderful walks straight from the cottage.”
